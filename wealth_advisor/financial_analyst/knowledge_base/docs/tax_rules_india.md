# Mutual Fund & Investment Taxation Basics (India)

> Educational reference content, not registered investment advice. Tax rules
> change with each Union Budget — verify current rates with a CA before
> acting, and never treat this file as a substitute for one.

## Equity vs. Debt: Why the Split Matters

Mutual funds are taxed differently depending on how much of the portfolio
sits in domestic equity:

- **Equity-oriented fund** — at least 65% invested in domestic equity shares.
  Covers most Large Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap, Flexi Cap, Focused, Value/Contra,
  and ELSS funds.
- **Debt-oriented / "specified mutual fund"** — domestic equity exposure of
  35% or less (per Section 50AA). Covers most Debt category funds (Liquid,
  Short/Medium/Long Duration, Corporate Bond, Gilt, etc.) and many
  Fund-of-Funds.
- Hybrid funds fall into one bucket or the other depending on their actual
  equity allocation, not their category label — check the scheme's stated
  equity % before assuming which tax treatment applies.

## Equity Fund Taxation

As of the July 2024 Union Budget changes (rates unchanged through the
2025 and 2026 Budgets):

- **Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG)** — units held **12 months or less**:
  taxed at a flat **20%**.
- **Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG)** — units held **more than 12 months**:
  taxed at **12.5%**, with the **first ₹1,25,000 of LTCG per financial year
  exempt** from tax entirely.
- No indexation benefit applies to equity fund gains at any holding period.

## Debt Fund Taxation

The rules split sharply based on **purchase date**, because the 2023 Finance
Act removed long-term treatment for debt funds bought afterward:

- **Units purchased on or after 1 April 2023** — all gains are treated as
  **short-term capital gains regardless of how long you hold them**, and are
  taxed at your **income tax slab rate**. There is no LTCG treatment and no
  indexation for these units, even if held for many years.
- **Units purchased before 1 April 2023** — if held for **more than 24
  months**, gains qualify for LTCG treatment at a **12.5% concessional rate**
  (per the 2024 Budget amendment). Indexation is **no longer available**,
  even for these grandfathered units — the old "20% with indexation" regime
  has been phased out.
- If held 24 months or less, pre-April-2023 units are taxed as STCG at slab
  rate.

**What indexation used to mean**: it let you inflate your original purchase
cost using a government cost-inflation index before computing the gain,
lowering the taxable profit. It no longer applies to debt fund gains under
current rules — don't assume it when estimating post-tax returns.

## Section 80C and ELSS

- **Section 80C** allows a deduction of up to **₹1,50,000 per financial
  year** from taxable income for eligible investments, including ELSS
  (Equity-Linked Savings Scheme) funds, PPF, life insurance premiums, and
  others. (Note: Section 80C is being renumbered to **Section 123** effective
  FY 2026-27 under the new Income Tax Act structure — the ₹1.5 lakh limit and
  ELSS eligibility are unaffected by the renumbering itself.)
- **ELSS lock-in** is **3 years from the date of investment (per SIP
  instalment, if invested via SIP)** — the shortest lock-in of any Section
  80C instrument (compare PPF's 15-year tenure or 5-year tax-saving FDs).
- Because ELSS is equity-oriented, gains realized after the lock-in are taxed
  under the **equity LTCG rules above** (12.5% above the ₹1.25 lakh
  exemption) — the 80C deduction is on the *investment*, not the *gain*.

## Practical Notes for Recommendations

- Always ask which bucket a fund falls in (equity vs. debt-oriented) before
  citing a tax rate — don't assume from the fund's name alone.
- Always ask the purchase date for debt fund holdings — pre- vs.
  post-April-2023 units in the *same scheme* can be taxed completely
  differently.
- Never state a net-of-tax return as guaranteed; tax rates, exemption
  thresholds, and holding-period rules are set by the Union Budget and can
  change year to year.
- This file is a reference for the assistant's own reasoning about tax
  treatment — always add the disclaimer that specific tax planning should be
  confirmed with a chartered accountant (CA) before the user acts on it.

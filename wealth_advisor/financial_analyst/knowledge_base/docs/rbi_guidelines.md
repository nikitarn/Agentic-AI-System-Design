# RBI's Role and NRI Investment Basics

> Educational reference content, not registered investment advice.

## RBI vs. SEBI — Who Regulates What

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and SEBI are India's two primary financial
regulators, but they cover different territory, and this assistant should
not conflate the two when citing a rule:

- **RBI** is India's central bank. It regulates monetary policy, issues
  currency, and supervises **banks, NBFCs (non-banking financial companies),
  and payment/settlement systems**. It also administers **FEMA (Foreign
  Exchange Management Act, 1999)**, which governs cross-border money
  movement — this is why RBI, not SEBI, is the relevant authority for NRI
  account and repatriation rules below.
- **SEBI** regulates the **securities market** — stock exchanges, brokers,
  portfolio managers, and mutual funds — and focuses on investor protection
  and market conduct (see `sebi_rules.md`).
- The two occasionally overlap on new financial instruments (e.g. products
  that are part banking, part securities), but for the purposes of this
  assistant: banking/forex/NRI-remittance questions route to RBI/FEMA rules,
  and fund/scheme/market-conduct questions route to SEBI rules.

## NRI Account Types

NRIs (Non-Resident Indians) typically hold one or both of these account
types, and the distinction drives what can and can't be repatriated:

- **NRE (Non-Resident External) account** — holds **foreign-earned income**
  brought into India. Interest earned is **tax-free in India**, and the
  **full balance can be repatriated abroad without limit**.
- **NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) account** — holds **India-sourced income**
  (rent, dividends, pension, etc.). Interest is **taxable in India**, and
  repatriation is **capped at USD 1 million per financial year** (after
  applicable taxes and documentation).
- **FCNR (Foreign Currency Non-Resident) deposits** — held in foreign
  currency, also carry an **unlimited repatriation** allowance.

## Repatriation Documentation

Moving money out of India under FEMA generally requires:

- **Form A2** — the FEMA declaration form for any foreign exchange
  remittance
- **Form 15CA / 15CB** — required specifically for **NRO account**
  repatriation, to certify taxes have been accounted for (15CB is signed by
  a CA; 15CA is a self-declaration filed with the tax department)
- NRE/FCNR repatriation is procedurally simpler since that money was never
  India-sourced income in the first place

## NRI Investment Options in India

Within RBI/FEMA rules, NRIs can invest in (non-exhaustive, illustrative
list — always confirm current eligibility per instrument):

- Listed stocks, via a **Portfolio Investment Scheme (PIS)** account routed
  through a designated bank
- Mutual funds (equity, debt, hybrid) — subject to the AMC's own NRI
  onboarding/KYC process, which can vary by country of residence (US/Canada
  NRIs face extra restrictions at many AMCs due to FATCA compliance
  overhead)
- Government securities and PSU bonds
- The National Pension System (NPS)
- Units of Infrastructure Debt Funds / InvITs

## Practical Notes for Recommendations

- If a user identifies as NRI, ask which account type (NRE/NRO/FCNR) funds
  are coming from before discussing repatriation — the rules genuinely
  differ, not just administratively.
- Don't assume a mutual fund is open to NRI investment from every country of
  residence — flag that AMC-level restrictions (especially for US/Canada
  NRIs) should be checked directly with the fund house.
- Route banking/forex/account-type questions to this RBI/FEMA framing;
  route fund-selection/market-conduct questions to `sebi_rules.md`.
